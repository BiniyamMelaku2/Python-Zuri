Solomon Popoli Linda

The man behind the popular song "The Lion Sleeps Tonight"





Linda was a South African musician, singer and composer best known as the composer of the song "Mbube", which later became the popular music success "The Lion Sleeps Tonight", and gave its name to the Mbube style of isicathamiya a cappella later popularized by Ladysmith Black Mambazo. "Mbube" is Zulu for "lion". The whole story of the song has been described by Rian Malan in "Where Does the Lion Sleep Tonight?".

Linda's time lines

  • 1909: Linda was born in Msinga in rural KwaZulu Natal
  • 1920: Linda, who could barely read English, composed and wrote the song 'Mbube'
  • 1930: Linda moved to Johannesburg where he worked at Mayi Mayi furniture shop
  • 1933: started a group known as Solomon Linda and the Evening Bird started to work at the Carlton Hotel
  • 1938: Linda and the Evening Birds recorded several songs in Eric Gallo's recording studios
  • 1939: 'Mbube' was recorded in four takes and the song was officially released
  • 1948: Linda sold the worldwide rights to 'Mbube' to a South African record label for $2
  • 1949: 'Mbube' had sold over 100,000 copies, making Linda a musical star in South Africa.
  • 1959: while performing on stage with The Evening Birds, Linda collapsed and was later diagnosed with kidney disease
  • 1962: Linda passed without receiving royalty for his works.
  • 2004: the descendants of Solomon Popoli Linda filled a lawsuit against the Walt Disney company, seeking $1.6 million in royalties.

Once upon a time, a long time ago, a small miracle took place in the brain of a man named Solomon Linda. It was 1939, and he was standing in front of a microphone in the only recording studio in black Africa when it happened. He hadn't composed the melody or written it down or anything. He just opened his mouth and out it came, a haunting skein of fifteen notes that flowed down the wires and into a trembling stylus that cut tiny grooves into a spinning block of bees wax, which was taken to England and turned into a record that became a very big hit in that part of Africa.
